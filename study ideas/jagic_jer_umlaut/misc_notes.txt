spelligns like Zogr. кождо < *kъ-žьdo are pretty convincing evidence of complete jer-shift since they are relatively "opaque"; though we have here a regular masc. nom. sg. -ъ ending, due to the suffix and the ensuing vocalisation of this morphological-ending-jer it changes to /o/ which is incongruous with the /zero/ morpheme that would have signalled masc. nom. sg. in post-Jer Shift zogr
Spelligns like Zogr. бьдръ which seem to show jer-umlaut even in strong position must be caused by infection from roots like бьдэти < *bъd- and are thus good arguments for the reality of a jer-umlaut, PACE Leskien
